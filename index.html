Happiness isn't the absence of problems, but rather the discovery of motivation, meaningful connections, a sense of fulfillment, and unwavering energy to overcome life's obstacles.

Dopamine

Dopamine, known as one of the happiness hormones, stimulates feelings of pleasure and satisfaction. It's linked to our reward system, which is why we feel so happy when we reward ourselves with something we enjoy.

Oxytocin

Oxytocin, often referred to as the love hormone, promotes feelings of trust and bonding. It is associated with our social interactions, and that's why we experience such joy when we connect with people we care about.

Serotonin

Serotonin, frequently called the mood stabilizer, fosters a sense of well-being and happiness. It's tied to our sense of balance and peace, which is why we feel so content when our life is in harmony and order.

Endorphin

Endorphin is the substance produced by our own body and is mainly released when we engage in physical activity. When released, it is capable of generating feelings of pleasure, good mood, and relaxation.
